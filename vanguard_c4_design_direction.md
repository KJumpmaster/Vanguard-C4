# Vanguard C4 Homepage Design Direction

## Strategic Positioning

The homepage should present **Vanguard C4** as the umbrella command suite, not as a loose collection of tools. The visitor should immediately understand that Vanguard C4 is intended to help a squadron operate with greater tactical discipline, planning clarity, and operational coordination. The site should not frame the suite as a spreadsheet replacement. It should frame the suite as a **mission command environment** for squadrons that want to plan, brief, task, execute, and improve.

> **Vanguard C4 is a squadron command suite built to turn planning, coordination, and tactical execution into one organized operating picture.**

## First-Screen Recommendation

The cinematic should remain, but it should become a controlled atmospheric layer rather than the entire message. The current page makes the visitor work too hard to understand what the product is. The redesign should put **Vanguard C4** at the top of the hierarchy, use `Into the Savage Skies — We Ascend` as a smaller motto, and then state the value proposition in plain operational language.

A strong first impression should include a clear headline, a crisp subheadline, and three immediate module cards. The user should see the possibilities for their squadron in the first few seconds.

## Proposed Homepage Copy

### Hero

**Vanguard C4**

**Build the tactical operations center for your squadron with Vanguard C4.**

SkyPilot, Norden 2, and Scout form a complete tactical operations suite for squadrons that want to plan smarter, brief faster, coordinate clearly, and operate with purpose.

**Into the Savage Skies — We Ascend**

### Module Framing

| Module | Role | Homepage Message |
|---|---|---|
| **SkyPilot** | Squadron operations and command picture | Build the operating picture, organize squadron activity, and keep pilots aligned before the first aircraft leaves the ground. |
| **Norden 2** | Strike planning and release analysis | Turn attack planning into repeatable tactical data with strike profiles, drop analysis, and mission-ready calculations. |
| **Scout** | Reconnaissance, tasking, and progression awareness | Replace the old Air Tasking Order card with a scouting and tasking module that helps teams find objectives, track priorities, and coordinate action. |

## Visual Direction

The visual style should remain cinematic and military aviation focused, but the page should become more polished and readable. The design should use the existing banner video as a background layer or hero visual, with a darker overlay, sharp typography, and mission-console accents. The product cards should look like operational panels rather than generic tiles.

## Content Structure

The redesigned page should include a compact navigation bar, a strong hero section, three product-module panels, a section explaining why the suite matters, a simple operational workflow, and a final call-to-action. The call-to-action should encourage the visitor to explore the suite rather than imply the application itself lives on this marketing page.

## Opinionated Cleanup Guidance

The homepage should avoid old labels such as `Combined Air Operations Center` as the primary title, because that competes with the Vanguard C4 brand. It should also avoid the old acronym, because that wording can distract from the serious tactical positioning. The stronger phrase is **Tactical Operations Center**, framed as something the squadron builds and operates through Vanguard C4. The old `AIR TASKING ORDER` label should be replaced by **Scout**. The site should also avoid legacy War Thunder data wording on the marketing surface, even if some of those files remain in the repository for now.
